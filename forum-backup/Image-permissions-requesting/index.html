For #1 and #3, the Content ToU (there’s a link to it if you navigate to the GW2 asset kit page under the Media navbar up top) provides sufficient “written permission” so long as you follow the parameters set forth within the ToU:

Guild Wars 2 Content Terms of Use
We reserve the right, at our sole discretion, to change, modify, add, or remove portions of these Content Terms of Use, at any time. It is your responsibility to check these Content Terms of Use periodically for changes. Your continued use of Guild Wars or Guild Wars 2 content, APIs, or materials following the posting of changes will mean that you accept and agree to the changes.
I’m interested in using Guild Wars or Guild Wars 2 art on a website, application, in a project, or on a Guild Wars, Guild Wars 2 or ArenaNet fansite. What is your policy regarding copyrights and trademarks? May I use ArenaNet, Guild Wars 2 and/or Guild Wars content, such as images, text, sounds, or video?
Yes, you may use our content-that is images, text, video and audio files-within the following parameters:
The content must be used in a non-commercial context for private, personal use only (i.e. not for use by a company)
You must include all copyright and other notices associated with the content
You acknowledge and agree that no title nor any other form of ownership to the content has been or will be transferred to you from ArenaNet nor from anyone else
You agree that you will not alter, disassemble, decompile, reverse-engineer or in any other way modify the content
You agree that you will not present the content as officially sanctioned or professionally associated with ArenaNet
You agree that ArenaNet has the right to require removal of our content at any time, for any reason

Bolding is from the page, just had to add formatting. Anyways, basically, as long as you arent selling the application (a donation button is a verrrrrrry hazy gray area that Gaile Gray would likely know, or can get the answer to quickly), provide proper attribution to the source, and not imply it’s anything “official,” Anet generally wont care. Lawton’s probably the same, until you try to make a program that sends billions of requests for every iota of information available from the api server about Grumble Cakes.

Yes, I’m aware this is an old thread, but the information added bears mentioning, as it’s still a valid concern for others than the OP.
